1. Overall, how effectively did your team work together on this project?

  • Poorly

2. What percentage of your team participated actively and was fully prepared most of the time?

A solid 50%. David was the only other member who showed up to any meetings - he was almost always prepared for the work we had to do.

3. Give one specific example of something you learned from the team that you probably would not have learned working alone.

David had a better understanding of "what looks good" than I did. He pushed me to make tougher changes to our design that I wasn't sure we could pull off.

The menu, for example. In my original Figma mock-up, the menu would have been just a simple grid. David wanted to make it look more like a real restaurant menu - because of this, we ended up with a much more complicated design and I feel I learned a lot about nesting grids/flex boxes because of it.

4. Give one specific example of something the other team members learned from you that they probably would not have learned otherwise.

When we started, David claimed he had no idea how to make Javascript work with a web page. I hope I was able to teach him a little bit about how it works. (Though my understanding is not much better yet, plenty to learn!)

5. Suggest at least one change the team could make to improve its performance or the project.

I feel our performance could have been increased by having the ability to work on the same code without needing to send files back and forth to each other. We could have both made edits whenever we had an idea.

As it turns out, this technology totally exists in the form of a Visual Studio Code Extension - Live Share. I really wish I had thought to look for it during the actual project.

Self Reflection

1. What did you learn from the team sprint experience?

I learned that I needed even more practice with HTML and CSS. I had done all of the work we were given for/in class each day, but I definately needed more.

Even in doing this project, there's a clear difference in my skill and comfort level with everything from when I started to where I finished. I just wish I could've gone through the whole thing with what I've learned now!

I have learned how to use Grid and Flex Box a lot better than I did before.

2. What do you think went well?

The Javascript worked well. I was glad that I was able to make it dynamic - though I'm sure there's a more efficient way I could have done it.

I also felt like the Figma portion of our project was very effective - David and I were able to discuss our ideas and pick the parts we liked the most.

3. What would you have done differently, given the opportunity?

I would have given the restaurant more personality. I was hoping to have time at the end of the project to really customize it and make it unique, but there just wasn't time.

I would have also gone through the entire HTML code with a fine comb, looking for ways to improve our use of semantic tags. Everything felt like sucha rush - I really didn't make enough time for it.

Oh, and I would have changed the cursor on the menu page to be a pointer when hovering over the menu items. I don't know how I forgot that.

4. Do you have any other comments or suggestions about the project?

Comment: It was tough with only two of us. We got through it, but I would love to see what we could've come up with if we had double the manpower.

Comment: I spent the first couple of days feeling like I was just getting things to work through trial and error. After all this practice, though, I'm able to do up this assessment page without any difficulty at all. Progression feels good!

Suggestion: Telling the students about the Live Share extension might be a good idea? I haven't tested it myself yet, but if it really does allow for multiple people to work simultaneously, it'd be invaluable.
